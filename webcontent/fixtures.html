Brief History about World Cup in Brazil

The FIFA Football World Cup is an international sporting event which takes place once in every four years where 32 teams battle it out for the coveted prize, the World Cup trophy.

FIFA World Cup 2014 is the 20th edition of the competition which began way back in 1930.

The 2014 edition is being hosted by Brazil, five time champions of the world. This is the second time the event is being organised in Brazil, the previous one being in 1950 in which Brazil finished runners up behind Uruguay.

The final of the 1950 World Cup was held in the Maracana stadium, Rio De Janeiro in which 173,850 fans witnessed Uruguay beat the home team Brazil 2-1.

The recorded 173,850 attendance was the highest ever for a football match.

This year also the final will be held in the same Maracana stadium which underwent renovations leading up to the tournament.

Brazil have won this tournament five times, more than any other country, their most recent win being in 2002 when the tournament was held for the first time in Asia in South Korea and Japan.
